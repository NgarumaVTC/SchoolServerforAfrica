* Case: ATX Midi-Tower (Local Procurement)
- *Part Number:* Generic (local procurement on-site)
- *Unit Price:* approx. 170.00 € (incl. local assembly by InnoSykes Computer, Moshi)
- *Quantity:* 1

** Why this part?
Transporting a PC case from Germany to Tanzania in checked luggage means paying to
ship empty space. The case is sourced brand-agnostic through the local computer shop,
which also handles professional assembly and initial functional testing.

Technical mandate for the shop: mesh front design with accessible dust filters;
internal positive air pressure via asymmetrical fan configuration; clearance for a
158 mm CPU cooler; minimum two front fans.

** Assembly Note
The server case is wall-mounted with significant clearance from the floor, secured
with a padlock and chain.

** Maintenance Note
Dust filters must be cleaned on a regular schedule. Frequency depends on observed
dust accumulation — check monthly, clean as needed.

* Power: UPS 750VA Line-Interactive (Local Procurement)
- *Reference:* APC Smart-UPS SMT750IC
- *Unit Price:* approx. 400.00 € (budget placeholder for local market)
- *Quantity:* 1

** Why this part?
Lead-acid batteries cannot be transported by air (hazmat regulations). The UPS is
sourced locally. Line-interactive with AVR (Automatic Voltage Regulation) is
mandatory to smooth out Tanzanian grid fluctuations. The primary role is not hours
of runtime but absorbing voltage spikes and bridging blackouts long enough for an
automated clean shutdown — essential for protecting the ZFS pool.

APC Smart-UPS is specified as the lead brand for best Linux/NUT compatibility.

** Purchasing Spec (If APC unavailable)
Line-Interactive, minimum 750VA, AVR, and a physical communication port (USB or
RJ45) for OS shutdown signal. Acceptable alternatives: CyberPower, Eaton.

* Power: Surge Protector Power Strip
- *Reference:* Brennenstuhl Premium-Protect-Line or APC SurgeArrest
- *Unit Price:* approx. 40.00 €
- *Quantity:* 1 (recommend 2 including a cold spare)

** Why this part?
Acts as the "electrical sacrificial anode" upstream of the UPS. In the event of a
catastrophic voltage spike (lightning, grid fault), this absorbs the destructive
energy first — protecting the 400 EUR UPS. If it triggers, only a cheap power strip
needs replacing.

* Motherboard: ASRock Rack X570D4U (AMD AM4)
- *Part Number:* X570D4U
- *Unit Price:* approx. 377.00 €
- *Quantity:* 1

** Why this part?
A dedicated server motherboard with IPMI/BMC enables full out-of-band management.
Even if NixOS crashes or the machine is powered off, the entire server can be
diagnosed, hard-reset, or maintained at BIOS level from Germany.

The model without integrated 10G copper (2L2T) was chosen deliberately: 10GBASE-T
generates significant heat and power draw. Instead, an SFP+ card provides 10G via
DAC cable — thermally and energetically superior.

** Alternatives
ASUS Pro WS X570-ACE (no out-of-band management)

* CPU: AMD Ryzen 9 5950X (16C/32T)
- *Part Number:* 100-100000059WOF
- *Unit Price:* 360.00 €
- *Quantity:* 1

** Why this part?
The AM4 platform is technologically mature, offering the best price/performance ratio.
Crucially, AM4 supports ECC memory natively — without the significant premium that
Intel Xeon platforms demand for ECC compatibility.

16 cores / 32 threads provide substantial headroom for running 20 concurrent RDP
desktop sessions plus all network services simultaneously. The CPU is configured at
65W TDP (Eco-Mode) in the BIOS for reduced thermal load and reduced strain on the
UPS and power grid — performance remains far more than sufficient at this limit.

** Alternatives
- AMD Ryzen 7 PRO 4750G (integrated GPU, fewer threads)
- AMD Ryzen 9 3900X (previous generation, lower efficiency)

* Cooling: Noctua PWM Case Fans (120/140mm)
- *Part Number:* NF-A14 PWM / NF-P12 redux (depending on case)
- *Unit Price:* approx. 20.00 € each
- *Quantity:* Maximum configuration of the chosen case (approx. 3–5 units)

** Why this part?
Fan choice is a critical long-term reliability decision. Noctua SSO2 bearings are
rated for extreme longevity under dust. Fully populating all fan mounts creates
positive internal air pressure — cooling air enters only through the dust filters,
minimising unfiltered ingress of fine red sand. PWM control reduces mechanical wear
further by matching fan speed to actual thermal load.

** Alternatives
Arctic P12/P14 PWM (cheaper, but lower expected bearing life under high dust load)

* RAM: 32GB DDR4-3200 ECC UDIMM (×2, 64 GB total)
- *Unit Price:* approx. 190.00 € (market price)
- *Quantity:* 2 (initial); expandable to 4 × 32 GB = 128 GB

** Why this part?
ECC (Error Correction Code) RAM is mandatory for stable ZFS operation. ZFS relies on
memory integrity; silent RAM errors without ECC can lead to data corruption that ZFS
itself cannot detect or prevent.

64 GB in Phase 1 comfortably runs 20 RDP desktop sessions plus all container
services. By using 32 GB modules in Phase 1 rather than 4 × 16 GB, all RAM slots
remain free for a straightforward Phase 2 expansion to 128 GB — without discarding
any existing modules.

Procurement is manufacturer-agnostic: Samsung, Hynix, Micron, Kingston — any module
meeting JEDEC DDR4-3200 ECC UDIMM specification is acceptable.

* Storage Tier 1 (System + Critical Data): Samsung SM883 480GB × 3
- *Part Number:* MZ7KH480HAHQ-00005
- *Unit Price:* approx. 110.00 €
- *Quantity:* 3

** Why this part?
Three datacenter-grade SSDs in a ZFS 3-way mirror provide exceptional fault
tolerance: any two drives can fail simultaneously and the pool remains intact.
Power-Loss-Protection (PLP) via integrated capacitors protects the NixOS system
and critical databases during sudden power events.

Logical partition: 128 GB per drive dedicated to the NixOS system; remaining
capacity for admin and teacher home directories, and mission-critical data.

** Alternatives
Intel S4610, Micron 5200 MAX
